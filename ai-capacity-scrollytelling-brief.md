# Scrollytelling Editorial Blueprint – AI Capacity Constellations

This document summarizes the narrative sections of the scrollytelling experience, the key messages each section must deliver, and the core UX/animation behaviors that bring those messages to life.

---

## Section 1 – Hero: Monolith → Constellation

### Key message
- Policy debates often treat **“capacity to benefit from AI” as a single stock** of skills, infrastructure, and institutional strength that can simply be increased. [file:1]
- The site’s thesis is that AI capacity is **not a monolith**; it is a **constellation of distinct but interacting dimensions**.

### UX and animation
- Initial visual: a single **solid block** labeled “AI Capacity”, feeling heavy and singular – the monolith.
- As the user scrolls, **hairline cracks** appear on the block; subtle vibration suggests the idea is under strain.
- The headline text changes from **“AI Capacity Is a Monolith”** to **“AI Capacity Is NOT a Monolith”** by inserting the word **“NOT” inside the existing phrase**, not by replacing the whole line.
- The block **implodes inward** (not explosively), fragments moving outwards and fading.
- From the implosion emerges a **soft central node** labeled “Capacity to Benefit from AI”, surrounded by **five named nodes**:
  - Access to AI Resources
  - Absorptive Capacity
  - Indigenous and Generative Capabilities
  - Institutional and Safeguarding Capacities
  - Digital Public Infrastructure and Data Ecosystems [file:1]
- Once the constellation is fully visible, a second line appears under the headline: **“It Is a Constellation of Capabilities.”**

## Section 2 – AccessDoorNotRoom

### Key message
- **Access** is the most basic dimension of AI capacity: connectivity, hardware, models/tools, and data. Without access, no other capacity can be exercised. [file:1]
- Access **lowers entry barriers** and can partially counter concentration of AI capabilities.
- Access is **necessary but insufficient**; it **opens the door** to AI, but does not determine whether communities can understand, integrate, or transform AI in ways that reflect their priorities. [file:1]

### UX and animation
- Visual metaphor: a **door labeled “Access to AI Resources”** that is slightly ajar.
- As the user scrolls, the door **splits into distinct panels/icons**, each representing a component of access:
  - Connectivity & hardware (networks, devices, data centers).
  - Models & tools (APIs, open source models, licensed platforms).
  - Data (high‑quality datasets, including local languages and contexts, with consent/sharing rules). [file:1]
- Each panel **lights up or slides out** as the corresponding paragraph enters view.
- The final beat shows the door still at the threshold, with a barely furnished interior behind it, reinforcing the idea: access is the door, **not the room**.

## Section 3 – AbsorptiveRecognizeAssimilateApply

### Key message
- **Absorptive capacity** is the ability to **recognize** the value of external AI‑related knowledge, **assimilate** it into local routines, and **apply** it to productive ends. [file:1]
- It converts access into meaningful use but is **intermediary**, not the ultimate aim.

### UX and animation
- Visual metaphor: a **three‑step flow** or pipeline with stages:
  - Recognize External AI
  - Assimilate into Local Systems
  - Apply and Learn
- As the user scrolls, each stage **activates in sequence**:
  - Recognize: external “signals” move toward the first stage; relevant tools are identified.
  - Assimilate: those signals merge with existing workflow/infrastructure visuals.
  - Apply: outputs and feedback loops appear, showing refinement over time.
- The flow connects back toward the constellation, hinting that absorption sits between access and other capabilities.

## Section 4 – AbsorptionCritiqueFrame

### Key message
- An **absorption‑centric frame** focuses on how well actors uptake external, globally dominant AI platforms and solutions. [file:1]
- This introduces **exogenous bias**, casting local actors as recipients rather than creators.
- It **underplays indigenous and generative capabilities** and **neglects pluriversal futures** where societies may choose different AI pathways. [file:1]

### UX and animation
- Split visual:
  - Left: strong flow of branded/global AI icons into a container labeled “Local System (Receiver)”.
  - Right: emerging scene of local research, community experimentation, and cultural/linguistic AI work, labeled “Local System (Author)”. [file:1]
- At first, the **left side visually dominates**; as the critique unfolds, the **right side grows in weight and detail**, pushing back against the receiver framing.
- The transition creates emotional tension, then clarity: the user sees what is lost when “capacity” is equated with absorption.

## Section 5 – ConstellationOfCapabilities

### Key message
- AI capacity is best understood as a **constellation of interlinked dimensions**, not a single stock. [file:1]
- The core dimensions are:
  - Access to AI Resources
  - Absorptive Capacity
  - Indigenous and Generative Capabilities
  - Institutional and Safeguarding Capacities
  - Digital Public Infrastructure and Data Ecosystems [file:1]
- Absorptive capacity connects access to effective use and interacts with generative and institutional capacities.

### UX and animation
- Central **constellation diagram**: a core node “Capacity to Benefit from AI” with the five dimensions in orbit.
- As the user scrolls, nodes **enter one by one** and lines between them light up, showing how access feeds into absorption, absorption into generative capabilities, and all are embedded in institutions and DPI.
- Clicking or hovering on a node reveals a **short definition and example**, encouraging exploration without clutter.

## Section 6 – AgencyPathways

### Key message
- In a **pluriversal framing**, societies hold diverse visions of progress and wellbeing. There is no single “correct” AI pathway. [file:1]
- Capacity includes the ability to **adopt, adapt, contest, co‑create, or resist** specific AI deployments.
- Agency means being able to choose among these pathways and co‑create alternative AI systems and governance models grounded in local knowledge and values. [file:1]

### UX and animation
- Visual metaphor: a **branching path diagram** starting from “Encounter with AI” and extending into five labeled routes:
  - Adopt
  - Adapt
  - Contest
  - Co‑create
  - Resist
- Hover/tap on each path reveals a **brief vignette** illustrating that pathway (e.g., contesting a surveillance system, co‑creating an agricultural tool). [file:1]
- All paths are visually equal; none is highlighted as the default or optimal route.

---

## Section 7 – SafeguardsAndInstitutions

### Key message
- **Institutional and safeguarding capacities** translate access and absorption into **just, rights‑respecting outcomes**. [file:1]
- They include policy design and coordination, regulation and enforcement, and social safeguards such as participatory mechanisms and independent oversight. [file:1]

### UX and animation
- Visual metaphor: a **layered system** with three levels:
  - Inner: AI projects and technologies.
  - Middle: policies, regulators, data governance bodies.
  - Outer: social safeguards and oversight.
- As the user scrolls, the view **zooms out**, revealing each additional layer.
- Small “risk” icons travel toward the center and are **caught or deflected** by outer layers, showing safeguards as enabling structures that manage harms rather than simply blocking innovation.

---

## Section 8 – PluriversalClosing

### Key message
- Capacity to benefit from AI cannot be captured by monolithic stock metaphors or absorptive receptivity alone. It is a **constellation** of dimensions. [file:1]
- Absorptive capacity is central but **intermediary**; the ultimate goal is to build ecosystems that can **selectively absorb, critically evaluate, and generatively transform AI** in line with diverse development visions. [file:1]
- True capacity lies in **connected, generative, safeguarded ecosystems** that enable societies to choose, contest, and co‑create AI trajectories aligned with their own values and aspirations. [file:1]

### UX and animation
- Visual metaphor: **multiple glowing paths** extending into different stylized landscapes (urban, rural, low‑tech, high‑tech), none presented as uniquely correct.
- Scroll slows and motion softens, inviting reflection rather than further exploration.
- Final screen centers one line, e.g.:
  - “The capacity to benefit from AI lies in ecosystems that can selectively absorb, critically evaluate, and generatively transform AI in line with pluriversal futures.”
- Optionally, a small control lets the reader **restart the story**, reinforcing the idea that understanding capacity is an ongoing process.
